Goal: to understand the effect of optimization on the landscape of trained neural networks

Meaning of "effect of optimization." What should we change?
- We will fix the architecture. Shallow CNN. Define cnn_block = (conv-relu-pool):
    cnn_block-cnn_block-cnn_block-flatten-dense-dense-softmax
- We will fix the overall optimizer: AdamW, which features decay rates for gradient moments + weight decay
- Why PWCCA? Why not direct comparison of weights or activations? A: It is not guaranteed (in fact, not expected)
that two identical NNs (even under the same ordering of training data) will converge to networks with similar
weights. Rather, what we might expect is that the *representations* that the layers are learning are similar.
This can be quantified by using canonical correlations between layers, rather than say, the Frobenius distance.

May 11: so the PWCCA paper (Morcos, Raghu, Bengio 2018) had already considered the effect of learning rate on the
space of converged neural networks. They found that trained neural networks (with varying initialization) clustered
based on their learning rates. This establishes the fact that (1) learning rate affects the final trained neural
network and (2) that the space of trained neural networks satisfies some kind of locality: similarly-trained neural
networks are "closer" in representation space.

Therefore, as a follow-up question: what is the effective dimension of this space? I.e. we have the Hyperparameters
(1) batch_norm / learning rate (scaled linearly) (2) weight_decay (3) beta1 (4) beta4. But my guess is that the actual
space of neural networks has lower effective dimension.

Natural follow-up: what is the theoretical explanation for this fact?

Natural follow-up II: how does the effective dimension change with time? with layer?
